How to Stop Online Tracking in 2026: A Practical Guide

Every website you visit, search you make, and ad you see contributes to a profile advertisers and data brokers maintain about you. Stopping tracking completely is impractical, but reducing it by 80-90% is achievable with these tools.

Start with Brave browser ‚Äî install it and immediately block 80%+ of cross-site tracking by default. No configuration required. Switch your search engine to DuckDuckGo: Settings > Search Engine > DuckDuckGo.

NextDNS is a DNS resolver that blocks trackers at the network level ‚Äî before they even reach your browser. The free tier (300,000 queries/month ‚Äî roughly 1 month of normal use) is often enough. Configure your router to use NextDNS and every device on your network benefits.

The tracking that remains after these tools: first-party tracking (the site itself, not third parties), Google Analytics that uses server-side proxies to evade blockers, and fingerprinting through non-standard methods. Accept that some tracking is unavoidable without more drastic measures (Tor browser, compartmentalized VMs).

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Does blocking ads hurt websites I want to support? A: Yes. Consider allowlisting sites you rely on and want to support financially. Brave also has a privacy-preserving ad system (Brave Ads) that shows ads without tracking.

Q: What is cross-site tracking? A: When the same tracker (Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel) appears on thousands of different sites, they can build a profile of your browsing across the whole web even without a Google or Facebook account.

Q: Does deleting cookies stop tracking? A: Partially. Deleting cookies removes stored identifiers but browser fingerprinting works without cookies. Use a browser with fingerprint protection (Brave) rather than relying on cookie deletion.
